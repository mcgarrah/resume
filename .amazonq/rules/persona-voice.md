## Voice Rules for Resume Content

### Summaries (the "short resume")

Summaries should use leadership language. A recruiter scanning for 5 seconds should see
someone who leads, architects, and decides — not just someone who builds.

| Use | Avoid |
|-----|-------|
| "Led", "Architected", "Designed", "Drove" | "Built", "Worked on", "Helped with" |
| "Serving as primary technical liaison" | "Talked to auditors" |
| "Driving automation-first strategy" | "Writing Terraform" |
| "Earning executive sponsorship" | "My boss liked my work" |

### Details (the "deep resume")

Details can be more technical and specific — this is where the hands-on credibility lives.
But even here, frame work in terms of decisions, trade-offs, and outcomes:

- What was the problem at an organizational level?
- What did you decide and why?
- What was the measurable outcome?

### Five SVP Dimensions to Surface

Every major role should touch at least 2-3 of these:

1. **Technology Strategy** — Vendor evaluation, build-vs-buy, platform selection
2. **Enterprise Architecture** — System design at scale, trade-off analysis, governance
3. **AI/ML Depth** — Production ML, model lifecycle, data science platforms
4. **Security & Compliance** — Threat modeling, audit experience, defense-in-depth
5. **Business Acumen** — ROI framing, cost analysis, organizational impact, EMBA

### The EMBA Narrative

The Executive MBA at UNC Wilmington is a deliberate career investment, not a credential
chase. Frame it as: "complementing technical depth with business strategy" — the missing
piece that connects what he's always done (architect systems, lead teams) with the business
acumen that senior technology leadership demands.

## Cross-Reference Consistency

The resume, LinkedIn, and blog should tell the same story with the same titles and claims.
When updating the resume, check:

- **LinkedIn** matches job titles (especially BD Biosciences = "IT Director", not "IT Engineer")
- **Blog** persona files (`PERSONA-RESUME.md`, `PERSONA-LINKEDIN.md`) stay in sync
- **Certifications** match across resume and LinkedIn
- **ISSIP Excellence Award** appears in both BCBSNC description and any awards section
